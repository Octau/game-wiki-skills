## Intro

Abilities are ways to deal damage, buff, and debuff in combat at the cost of mana, or MP. Abilities are unlocked by using their respective ability book, which are all acquired as drops from monsters in combat or can be purchased from the Marketplace. Abilities get stronger as you level them up, increasing their effectiveness. Abilities that deal damage do so based on a flat amount, combined with a percentage of the damage dealt by the associated combat style.

## Ability Books

Ability books are consumable items that unlock abilities for use in combat. Once consumed, the ability is permanently learned and the book is consumed. There are 56 ability books divided into five tiers based on their level requirement.

### Color Coding

Ability books are color-coded by their ability type, making them easy to identify at a glance:

| Color | Ability Type | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Red** | Melee Abilities | Physical melee combat abilities (Stab, Slash, Smash styles) |
| **Green** | Ranged Abilities | Ranged weapon abilities (Bows, Crossbows) |
| **Purple** | Magic Abilities | Elemental magic abilities (Fire, Water, Nature) |
| **Yellow** | Buffing Abilities | Support abilities that buff allies or debuff enemies |
| **Blue** | Special Abilities | Special abilities that use the dedicated Special Ability slot |

### Tiers

Ability books have level requirements ranging from 1 to 60:

| Tier | Level Requirement | Number of Books | XP from Duplicate |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 1 | 8 | 50 XP |
| 2 | 20 | 11 | 500 XP |
| 3 | 40 | 11 | 500 XP |
| 4 | 50 | 10 | 500 XP |
| 5 | 60 | 16 | 500 XP |

**Acquisition:** Ability books are obtained as drops from monsters in combat or can be purchased from the Marketplace.

**Using Books:** Consuming a book you already know grants experience to that ability instead of unlocking it again.

### Regular Abilities vs Special Abilities

Abilities fall into two categories:

**Regular Abilities:** Can be equipped in your standard ability slots (1-4 slots depending on Intelligence level). Most damage, healing, and buff abilities fall into this category.

**Special Abilities:** Must be equipped in the dedicated Special Ability slot (separate from regular ability slots). Special abilities include:

* **Aura Abilities (Level 40):** Critical Aura, Fierce Aura, Guardian Aura, Mystic Aura, Speed Aura
* **Utility Abilities (Level 60):** Insanity, Invincible, Revive

All players have one Special Ability slot available at all Intelligence levels.

## Leveling

Abilities gain 0.1 XP every time they are used in combat. You can also gain a large amount of XP by consuming duplicate ability books. The XP granted depends on the book's level requirement:

* **Level 1 books**: 50 XP (Poke, Scratch, Smack, Quick Shot, Entangle, Fireball, Water Strike, Minor Heal)
* **Level 20+ books**: 500 XP (all other abilities)

Abilities increase in effectiveness as they level up, with scaling bonuses to damage, buff/debuff effects, and other properties.

## Ordering

Your Intelligence level determines how many abilities you can equip at once:

| Intelligence Level | Regular Ability Slots | Special Ability Slot |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1-19 | 1 | 1 |
| 20-49 | 2 | 1 |
| 50-89 | 3 | 1 |
| 90+ | 4 | 1 |

When multiple abilities are available for use during combat, they will be prioritized from left to right. This means that abilities to the right of those with zero cooldown will never activate, so zero cooldown abilities should always be as far right as possible.

## Ability Mechanics

### Ability Haste

Ability Haste reduces the cooldown duration of all abilities. Equipment and buffs can provide Ability Haste, allowing abilities to be used more frequently.

### Cast Speed

Cast Speed reduces the cast time of abilities. Higher Cast Speed from your  Attack level and equipment allows you to cast abilities faster, making you less vulnerable during casting.

### Conditional Triggers

Abilities can be configured with conditional triggers to activate automatically based on combat conditions such as:

* HP/MP thresholds (yours or allies')
* Presence or absence of buffs/debuffs
* Enemy count or status
* Cooldown availability

These triggers allow abilities to fire automatically without manual activation, enabling complex combat strategies.
